# B2 Upper-Intermediate Vocabulary

## Professional & Workplace
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| leverage | use to maximum advantage | "Let's leverage our existing tools" |
| streamline | make more efficient | "We need to streamline the onboarding" |
| scope | the range of a project | "That's out of scope for this quarter" |
| bandwidth | (also B1) capacity for work | "Do you have bandwidth for another project?" |
| bandwidth to grow | room to advance | "This role has a lot of bandwidth to grow" |
| buy-in | agreement/support | "We need leadership buy-in first" |

## Expressing Nuanced Opinions
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| on the fence | undecided | "I'm on the fence about the new policy" |
| trade-off | a balance achieved between two things | "It's a trade-off between speed and quality" |
| for what it's worth | acknowledging limited value | "For what it's worth, I agree with you" |
| arguably | possibly true | "That's arguably the best approach" |
| in hindsight | looking back | "In hindsight, we should have waited" |
| from my perspective | my viewpoint | "From my perspective, it's worth the risk" |

## Abstract Concepts
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| implication | likely consequence | "We need to think about the implications" |
| underlying | fundamental / hidden | "The underlying issue is trust" |
| premise | starting assumption | "The whole premise doesn't hold up" |
| tangible | concrete / measurable | "We need tangible results by Q3" |
| intangible | not easily measured | "Culture is intangible but critical" |
| inherent | built-in / essential | "There's inherent risk in any launch" |

## Hedging & Precision
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| tend to | usually do | "I tend to wake up early" |
| more or less | approximately | "We're more or less on schedule" |
| by and large | generally | "By and large, it went well" |
| in terms of | regarding | "In terms of cost, it's reasonable" |
| to some extent | partly | "To some extent, that's true" |

## Idiomatic & Conversational
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| under the weather | feeling sick | "I'm feeling a bit under the weather" |
| piece of cake | very easy | "That exam was a piece of cake" |
| break the ice | start conversation | "He told a joke to break the ice" |
| on the same page | in agreement | "Glad we're on the same page" |
| out of the blue | unexpectedly | "He called completely out of the blue" |
| give it a shot | try something | "Why not give it a shot?" |
