# 📞 Phone Calls

## Scenario 1: Booking an Appointment
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You're calling a clinic to book a doctor's appointment.

### Role Play
You are the patient. VoiceTutor plays the receptionist.

### Key Phrases
- "Hi, I'd like to book an appointment" — Standard opener
- "I'm calling to schedule..." — Alternative phrasing
- "Do you have anything this week?" — Time ask
- "Could I get something after 5?" — Specific window
- "That works for me" — Confirming

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "I want appointment" → ✅ "I'd like to book an appointment"
- ❌ "When you free?" → ✅ "When do you have openings?"
- ❌ "Tomorrow good?" → ✅ "Does tomorrow work?"

### Example Dialogue
> **Receptionist:** Family Health Clinic, this is Maria speaking.
> **You:** Hi, I'd like to book an appointment with Dr. Lee sometime this week.
> **Receptionist:** Sure! Dr. Lee has Thursday morning at 9 or Friday at 2. Which works better?
> **You:** Thursday at 9 works great.
> **Receptionist:** Perfect. Can I get your name and date of birth?

## Scenario 2: Customer Service Issue
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Setting:** You're calling about a billing error on your account.

### Role Play
You are the customer. VoiceTutor plays the customer service rep.

### Key Phrases
- "I'm calling about a charge on my account" — Direct opener
- "There seems to be an error" — Polite problem statement
- "Could you walk me through this charge?" — Investigating
- "I'd like to dispute this" — Formal escalation
- "Can you confirm that's been resolved?" — Final check

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "You charged me wrong" → ✅ "There seems to be a charge I don't recognize"
- ❌ "Fix this now" → ✅ "Could we look into this together?"
- ❌ "I want refund" → ✅ "I'd like to dispute this charge"

### Example Dialogue
> **Rep:** Thank you for calling BrightNet, this is Jordan. How can I help?
> **You:** Hi Jordan, I'm calling about a charge on my account that I don't recognize — $89 from last week.
> **Rep:** I'm sorry to hear that. Can I get your account number?
> **You:** Sure, it's 555-1234.
> **Rep:** Okay, I see the charge. Let me look into this for you...

## Scenario 3: Leaving a Voicemail
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You call someone and they don't pick up. You need to leave a clear message.

### Role Play
You are the caller. VoiceTutor evaluates your voicemail.

### Key Phrases
- "Hi [name], this is [your name]" — Identify yourself
- "I'm calling about..." — State the purpose
- "Could you call me back at...?" — Clear callback number
- "No rush, whenever you get a chance" — Low urgency
- "Thanks, talk soon!" — Friendly close

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Call me back" (no info) → ✅ "Could you call me back at 555-1234?"
- ❌ Speaking too fast → ✅ Slow down, repeat the number
- ❌ No clear reason → ✅ "I'm calling about the meeting tomorrow"
