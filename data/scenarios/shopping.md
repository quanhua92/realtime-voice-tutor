# 🛍️ Shopping & Retail

## Scenario 1: Returning an Item
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Setting:** You bought something that doesn't fit and want to return it.

### Role Play
You are the customer. VoiceTutor plays the store associate.

### Key Phrases
- "I'd like to return this" — Direct opener
- "It didn't quite work out" — Soft reason
- "Do you have the receipt?" — You'll hear this
- "Could I get a refund instead?" — Alternative to exchange
- "What's your return policy?" — Asking rules

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "I no want" → ✅ "I'd like to return this"
- ❌ "Money back now" → ✅ "Could I get a refund?"
- ❌ "It's bad" → ✅ "It didn't quite work out"

### Example Dialogue
> **Associate:** Hi! How can I help you today?
> **You:** Hi, I'd like to return this shirt. It didn't quite fit.
> **Associate:** No problem. Do you have the receipt?
> **You:** Yes, right here. Could I get a refund instead of store credit?
> **Associate:** Of course! It'll go back to your card in 3-5 days.

## Scenario 2: Asking for Help Finding Something
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Setting:** You're in a large store and can't find what you need.

### Role Play
You are the customer. VoiceTutor plays the store associate.

### Key Phrases
- "Excuse me, where can I find...?" — Polite ask
- "I'm looking for..." — Direct
- "Do you carry...?" — Asking if stocked
- "Is there more in the back?" — Hidden stock
- "Could you check for me?" — Asking for help

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Where is?" → ✅ "Where can I find...?"
- ❌ "You have?" → ✅ "Do you carry...?"
- ❌ "Get me one" → ✅ "Could you check if there are any in the back?"

### Example Dialogue
> **Associate:** Hi! Finding everything okay?
> **You:** Actually, I'm looking for a blue button-down shirt in medium. Where would that be?
> **Associate:** Men's shirts are in aisle 4. Let me show you.
> **You:** Thanks! Do you carry the brand Everlane?
> **Associate:** Hmm, let me check. I think there might be some in the back.

## Scenario 3: Polite Bargaining at a Market
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You're at a flea market or independent shop where prices are flexible.

### Role Play
You are the customer. VoiceTutor plays the vendor.

### Key Phrases
- "That's a bit more than I was hoping to spend" — Soft opening
- "Could you do...?" — Suggesting a price
- "What's your best price?" — Inviting an offer
- "I'll think about it" — Polite stall
- "If you can do X, I'll take it" — Conditional agreement

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Too expensive" → ✅ "That's a bit more than I budgeted"
- ❌ "Lower price" → ✅ "Could you do $40?"
- ❌ "No, thanks" (too final) → ✅ "Let me think about it"
