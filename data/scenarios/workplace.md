# 💼 Workplace & Office

## Scenario 1: Monday Morning Small Talk
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You arrive at the office and bump into a coworker.

### Role Play
You are yourself. VoiceTutor plays your coworker Alex.

### Key Phrases
- "How was your weekend?" — Classic opener
- "Not much, just..." — Low-key response
- "Did you end up...?" — Following up on plans
- "I totally meant to..." — Excuse for not doing something
- "Catch you at the meeting" — Natural exit

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "What did you do?" → ✅ "How was your weekend?"
- ❌ "I stayed home only" → ✅ "I mostly just stayed in"
- ❌ "See you" (too blunt) → ✅ "Catch you later" or "See you at the standup"

### Example Dialogue
> **Alex:** Hey! Morning! How was your weekend?
> **You:** Pretty chill, mostly just stayed in. You?
> **Alex:** Good! Went hiking on Saturday. Did you end up trying that new restaurant?
> **You:** Oh man, I totally meant to, but got lazy. Maybe this weekend.
> **Alex:** Haha, fair enough. Catch you at the standup!

## Scenario 2: Speaking Up in a Meeting
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Setting:** You're in a team meeting and want to share an idea.

### Role Play
You are a team member. VoiceTutor plays your manager.

### Key Phrases
- "I had a thought..." — Soft opener
- "Could we try...?" — Suggesting an idea
- "Building on what [name] said..." — Adding to others
- "One thing I'm wondering is..." — Raising a concern
- "Happy to take that on" — Volunteering

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "I think we should..." (too direct) → ✅ "Could we explore...?"
- ❌ "That's wrong" → ✅ "I see it a little differently"
- ❌ "I'll do it" (too eager) → ✅ "Happy to take that on if no one else has bandwidth"

### Example Dialogue
> **Manager:** So, any thoughts on how to improve onboarding?
> **You:** Yeah, I had a thought — could we add a buddy system for new hires?
> **Manager:** Interesting. How would that work?
> **You:** Pair them with someone outside their team for the first two weeks. Building on what Priya said about cross-team bonding, this could really help.
> **Manager:** I like it. Can you put together a quick proposal?
> **You:** Happy to take that on.

## Scenario 3: Following Up on an Email
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You sent an email and haven't heard back.

### Role Play
You are yourself. VoiceTutor plays your colleague Sam.

### Key Phrases
- "Just circling back on..." — Polite nudge
- "Wanted to follow up on..." — Direct
- "No rush, just checking" — Low pressure
- "Did you get a chance to...?" — Soft ask
- "Let me know if you need anything from me" — Helpful close

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Did you read my email?" → ✅ "Just wanted to follow up on my email"
- ❌ "Why no reply?" → ✅ "No rush, just checking in"
- ❌ "Answer me" → ✅ "Let me know your thoughts when you get a chance"
