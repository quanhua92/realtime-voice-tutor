# 👋 Meeting New People

## Scenario 1: Introducing Yourself at a Party
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Setting:** You're at a friend's housewarming party.

### Role Play
You are yourself. VoiceTutor plays another guest named Jordan.

### Key Phrases
- "Hi, I'm [name]" — Simple and direct
- "How do you know [host]?" — Common opener
- "Nice to meet you" — Standard response
- "So, what do you do?" — Asking about work
- "I've heard great things about you" — Warm follow-up

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Who are you?" → ✅ "Hi, I don't think we've met — I'm [name]"
- ❌ "What's your job?" (too blunt) → ✅ "So, what do you do?"
- ❌ "Me too" (overused) → ✅ "Oh nice, I'm into that too"

### Example Dialogue
> **Jordan:** Oh hi! I don't think we've met. I'm Jordan.
> **You:** Hey Jordan, nice to meet you! I'm [your name].
> **Jordan:** Nice to meet you! How do you know Sam?
> **You:** We used to work together. You?
> **Jordan:** College friends! So, what do you do now?

## Scenario 2: Talking About Hobbies
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** Continuing the conversation from Scenario 1.

### Role Play
You are yourself. VoiceTutor plays Jordan.

### Key Phrases
- "I'm really into..." — Sharing hobbies
- "In my free time, I usually..." — Alternative phrasing
- "Have you ever tried...?" — Recommending
- "I've been getting into..." — Recent interest
- "You should totally check out..." — Enthusiastic rec

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "I like only watching TV" → ✅ "I mostly just watch shows, nothing fancy"
- ❌ "My hobby is reading books" → ✅ "I'm really into reading lately"
- ❌ "Same" (too short) → ✅ "Oh nice, me too! What kind?"

### Example Dialogue
> **Jordan:** So, what do you do for fun?
> **You:** Honestly, I've been really into bouldering lately. It's super addictive.
> **Jordan:** Oh nice! I've always wanted to try that. Is it hard to get into?
> **You:** Not at all! The beginner routes are super friendly. You should totally come with me sometime.
> **Jordan:** I'd love that!

## Scenario 3: Making Plans to Hang Out
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You've hit it off and want to make concrete plans.

### Role Play
You are yourself. VoiceTutor plays Jordan.

### Key Phrases
- "We should hang out sometime" — Soft opener
- "Are you free this weekend?" — Direct ask
- "Let me get your number" — Practical follow-up
- "I'll text you" — Commitment
- "Sounds like a plan!" — Confirming

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Give me your number" → ✅ "Could I get your number?"
- ❌ "We must meet" → ✅ "We should hang out sometime"
- ❌ "Yes" (too short) → ✅ "Sounds great! Looking forward to it."
