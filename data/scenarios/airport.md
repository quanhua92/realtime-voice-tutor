# ✈️ Airport & Travel

## Scenario 1: Check-in Counter
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Setting:** You arrive at the airport for your flight.

### Role Play
You are the traveler. VoiceTutor plays the check-in agent.

### Key Phrases
- "I'd like to check in for my flight to..." — Polite opener
- "Here's my passport" — Handing over documents
- "Can I get a window seat?" — Seat preference
- "How many bags can I check?" — Baggage question
- "Is the flight on time?" — Status check

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "I want to fly" → ✅ "I'm here to check in"
- ❌ "Give me window" → ✅ "Could I get a window seat?"
- ❌ "Where I go?" → ✅ "Where do I need to go next?"

### Example Dialogue
> **Agent:** Good morning! Where are you flying today?
> **You:** Hi, I'm heading to Tokyo. Here's my passport.
> **Agent:** Thank you. Would you like a window or aisle seat?
> **You:** A window seat, please. Also, how many bags can I check?
> **Agent:** You get two checked bags. Anything else?
> **You:** No, that's everything. Thanks!

## Scenario 2: Asking for Directions at the Airport
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** You're lost trying to find your gate.

### Role Play
You are the traveler. VoiceTutor plays an airport staff member.

### Key Phrases
- "Excuse me, where is gate...?" — Direct ask
- "I'm looking for..." — Polite search
- "How do I get to...?" — Asking for directions
- "Is this the right way to...?" — Confirming direction
- "Could you point me to...?" — Slightly more polished

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Where gate?" → ✅ "Where is gate B12?"
- ❌ "I lost" → ✅ "I'm a bit turned around"
- ❌ "Show me" → ✅ "Could you point me in the right direction?"

### Example Dialogue
> **Staff:** Hi there, need any help?
> **You:** Yeah, I'm trying to find gate B12. Is this the right way?
> **Staff:** Almost! Take a left at the food court, then straight for about 5 minutes.
> **You:** Left at the food court, then straight. Got it. Thank you!

## Scenario 3: Dealing with a Delay
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Setting:** Your flight is delayed and you're stressed.

### Role Play
You are the traveler. VoiceTutor plays the gate agent.

### Key Phrases
- "What's the status of flight...?" — Direct ask
- "Do you have an updated ETA?" — When will it leave
- "Is there any compensation?" — For long delays
- "Can I get rebooked?" — Changing flights
- "What are my options?" — Open-ended ask

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ "Why late?" → ✅ "Could you tell me why we're delayed?"
- ❌ "I want money back" → ✅ "What are my options for compensation?"
- ❌ "Fix this" → ✅ "Is there anything that can be done?"
